\thesisabstract{This thesis explores coordinated multi-robot motion planning on a discrete grid. We first investigate minimum makespan motion planning, proving its decision problem is NP-complete by simplifying a reduction from previous work. We then provide an overview of two current approximation algorithms that efficiently compute schedules with a guaranteed level of robot performance. We finally extend a previous theorem to hold specifically on the grid, showing that optimizing different cost functions will result in different output schedules. Although our focus is limited to the discrete case, we acknowledge that more efficient schedules are possible in the continuous domain, but this domain is less well-understood. Overall, our thesis provides valuable insights into the field of coordinated motion planning on a discrete grid, paving the way for future research in this area.}
